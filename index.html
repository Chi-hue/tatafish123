    About Us

    It started, quite simply, like this...

    Tatafish Foods began with a vision shared by a couple who were passionate about healthy eating and African culinary traditions. Founded in 2006 by Mr. and Mrs. Chukwuemeka and Ijeoma Ogbonna in Owerri, Imo State, Nigeria, Tatafish Foods started with a simple yet powerful idea: to replace meat in traditional African dishes with organic, wholesome seafood.

    Inspired by the richness of Africa’s waters and its culinary heritage, we grew from a single location in Owerri to branches in Abuja, Calabar, and Port Harcourt. Today, we continue to offer meals that not only celebrate Africa’s diverse flavors but also promote a healthier lifestyle for our customers.

    At Tatafish Foods, we believe that food is more than nourishment—it’s a way to connect with our roots, our community, and our future. Each dish we serve tells a story of tradition, health, and sustainability, crafted with care and passion.

    Our Mission

    It started, quite simply, like this...

    What started as a heartfelt desire to bring healthier eating options to African kitchens has grown into a thriving culinary journey. Tatafish Foods was founded in 2006 by Mr. and Mrs. Chukwuemeka and Ijeoma Ogbonna, who saw an opportunity to transform the way Africans experience food. In a world where meat often dominates the plate, they envisioned a future where organic seafood takes center stage—delivering both flavor and wellness.

    From our humble beginnings in Owerri, Imo State, our mission has always been clear: to celebrate Africa’s culinary traditions while championing healthier, more sustainable food choices. With branches now in Abuja, Calabar, and Port Harcourt, we are proud to serve dishes that honor the rich biodiversity of Africa’s waters.

    At Tatafish Foods, our commitment is not just to feed but to uplift—creating meals that nourish the body, honor our cultural heritage, and inspire a healthier lifestyle. We invite you to join us in a movement that redefines African cuisine, one seafood dish at a time.
